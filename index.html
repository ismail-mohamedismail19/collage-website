The establishment of the university :

Bani Swief Technological University is a public university located in Bani Swief Governorate, Egypt. It was established in 2019. The university offers undergraduate and graduate programs in various fields, including engineering and computer science. Its mission is to provide quality education, conduct research, and contribute to the development of the local community. Bani Swief Technological University has modern facilities and state-of-the-art equipment, including labs and workshops, to support research and practical training. The university also has a number of research centers, such as the Center for Renewable Energy.

International cooperation between the university and the State of South Korea :

An agreement was signed between the Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research and the Korea International Cooperation Agency (KOICA). And obtaining accreditation for university programs that include the Mechatronics Program and the Information Technology (ICT) Program for a period of 6 years from 2022 to 2028.The university is committed to promoting internationalization and offers exchange programs and collaborations with other universities around the world. It also encourages entrepreneurship and innovation, providing support for startups and organizing various events and competitions to promote creativity and problem-solving skills among its students. Overall, Bani Swief Technological University is a dynamic and innovative institution that strives to prepare its graduates to be leaders in their fields and contribute to the development of society.

Acceptance of students :

The Egyptian-Korean College of Industry and Energy Technology accepts Egyptian students who have a certificate of completion of:
  • Technical secondary education system three and five years.
  • High school.
  • Technical institutes two-year system.
  • Professional diploma.
The student must meet the college admission requirements, which are:
  • The student obtains the minimum admission requirement for the college, which is determined by the Coordination Office or the Supreme Council of Universities.
  • Passing aptitude tests.
  • The student must be full-time for the college only.
  • Passing the college medical examination.
